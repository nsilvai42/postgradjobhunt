üß† Psych Tip of the Week: The "End of Sprint" Reflection

As we close out Sprint 2, it's easy to focus only on what's left to do. But taking a moment to reflect is one of the most powerful ways to make the *next* sprint even better.

Our brains naturally evolved to focus on what could go wrong. Celebrating a small win increases your belief in yourself, motivating your for your next challenge.

Take 60 seconds to answer these two questions:

  1. What's one thing I'm proud of from this sprint?
  2. What's one small thing I'll do differently in the next sprint?
    • ‚û°Ô∏è Deciding on a specific, small action turns a vague goal into a concrete plan, and makes you more likely to actually do it.

This simple exercise helps you recognize your progress, build momentum, and make your next sprint more focused and effective.
