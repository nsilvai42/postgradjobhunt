Practice Telling Your Story

An interview isn't just a test; it's a conversation where you get to tell your story. Companies don't hire resumes‚Äîthey hire people. Your unique experiences and perspective are what set you apart.

This week, we're focusing on turning interviews into offers. It's time to practice, prepare, and ace the interview!

Follow these steps to turn your experiences into compelling interview answers.

Step 1: Outline Your Key Stories.

  1. Brainstorm 3-5 key experiences from your resume.
  2. For each, use the STAR method (Situation, Task, Action, Result) to structure your story.
  3. Practice telling these stories out loud until they feel natural.

Step 2: Prepare for Common Questions.

  1. Draft answers to questions like "Tell me about yourself" and "Why are you interested in this role?" using the Interview Talking Points worksheet.
  2. Use AI tools to refine your answers and get feedback.
  3. Record yourself answering questions to check your pacing and body language.

üß† Psych Tip of the Week: Cognitive Reappraisal

Facing a tough interview or a rejection? How you frame it matters. Instead of seeing it as a threat ("I'm going to fail"), re-frame it as a challenge ("This is a chance to show my skills").

  1. Identify the negative thought: "I'm not qualified for this job."
  2. Challenge it and re-frame it: "This role is a stretch, which makes it a great opportunity to learn. I'll focus on the qualifications I *do* have."

This small mental shift can reduce anxiety and improve performance by turning stress into a source of energy.
